Welcome to my website! In December 2016, I finished my biomedical engineering graduate work under the mentorship of Dr. Shayn Peirce-Cottler and Dr. Silvia Blemker. Early in my career at the University of Virginia, I took numerous courses on computational modeling which sparked my interest in understanding biological phenomenon through models. In particular, I am fascinated by skeletal muscle and its ability to constantly adapt and remodel. I was able to combine these two passions during my graduate career by developing agent-based models of skeletal muscle atrophy and injury. Under the guidance of Dr. George Christ, my current post-doctoral work is wet-lab based, increasing my purview to include vascular and nerve adaptation following muscle injury. Please check out my research section for details on many of the projects I am involved in!
